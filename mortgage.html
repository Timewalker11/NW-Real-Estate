Mortgage & Financing

In-House Mortgage Support, Connected to Your Home Search

Understanding your financing options early makes the rest of the process easier. Here's an overview of common loan types and how the mortgage process generally works.

Loan Options

Common Types of Home Loans

The right loan depends on your finances, goals, and eligibility. These are general descriptions to help you start the conversation.

Conventional Loans

Not backed by a government agency, typically requiring a stronger credit profile and a down payment that can vary based on the lender and loan program.

FHA Loans

Backed by the Federal Housing Administration, often with more flexible credit requirements and lower minimum down payments.

VA Loans

Available to eligible veterans, service members, and certain surviving spouses, often with no down payment required.

Refinancing

Replacing an existing mortgage with a new one, which may help adjust your rate, term, or monthly payment depending on your situation.

The Process

How the Mortgage Process Generally Works

  1. Pre-Qualification

    Share basic financial information to get an early sense of what you may be able to borrow.

  2. Application

    Submit a full loan application along with supporting documentation such as income and asset verification.

  3. Processing & Underwriting

    Your file is reviewed and verified to confirm you meet the requirements for the loan program.

  4. Closing

    Sign final loan documents and complete the transaction, finalizing your financing.
