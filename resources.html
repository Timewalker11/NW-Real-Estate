Resources

Guides, Terms, and Answers to Common Questions

A few general resources to help you feel more prepared, whether you're just starting to think about a move or already deep in the process.

Guides

General Buyer & Seller Guides

Preparing to Buy a Home

Before you start touring homes, it generally helps to review your finances, understand your credit picture, and get a sense of your price range through pre-approval. Making a list of must-haves versus nice-to-haves can also keep your search focused once you start looking.

Preparing to Sell a Home

Sellers typically benefit from decluttering, making minor repairs, and reviewing recent comparable sales in their area before listing. A clear plan for showings and marketing helps set expectations for how the process will unfold.

Understanding Closing Costs

Both buyers and sellers typically pay some form of closing costs, which can include lender fees, title fees, taxes, and other charges. These vary by transaction, so it's worth reviewing an estimate early in the process.

What to Expect at a Home Inspection

A home inspection generally reviews the condition of a property's major systems and structure. The results can inform negotiations or highlight repairs to address before closing.

Glossary

Common Real Estate & Mortgage Terms

Pre-Approval
A lender's preliminary assessment of how much you may be able to borrow, based on a review of your financial information.
Appraisal
An independent estimate of a property's market value, typically required by a lender before finalizing a loan.
Escrow
A neutral third party that holds funds and documents during a transaction until agreed-upon conditions are met.
Down Payment
The portion of a home's purchase price paid upfront, rather than financed through the mortgage.
Underwriting
The lender's process of verifying your financial information and confirming you meet the requirements for a loan.
Closing Disclosure
A document provided before closing that outlines the final terms and costs of your loan.
Contingency
A condition in a purchase offer that must be met for the sale to proceed, such as a satisfactory inspection or financing approval.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be pre-approved before touring homes?

It's not always required, but getting pre-approved early generally gives you a clearer sense of your budget and can make your offer more competitive when you find the right home.

How long does a typical home purchase take?

Timelines vary widely depending on financing, negotiations, and local market conditions. We can talk through a more specific estimate based on your situation.

Can NW Real Estate and Mortgage help with both the real estate and financing side?

Yes — that's the reason the two services are offered together, so you have one point of contact for both parts of the process.

What if I'm just starting to think about buying or selling?

That's a good time to reach out. We're happy to answer questions and help you think through timing, even if you're not ready to move yet.
