Real Estate Glossary

Common Terms, Explained Simply

A quick reference for terms you'll run into while buying, selling, or financing a home.

ACD EFH LMP TU
A
Amortization
The process of paying off a loan over time through regular payments that cover both principal and interest.
APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The yearly cost of a loan including interest and certain fees, expressed as a percentage.
Appraisal
A licensed appraiser's professional opinion of a property's market value, required by most lenders.
C
Closing Costs
Fees paid at closing beyond the purchase price, including lender fees, title insurance, and escrow charges — typically 2-5% of the loan amount.
Contingency
A condition in a purchase contract that must be met, such as a satisfactory inspection or loan approval, or the buyer can cancel.
Comparative Market Analysis (CMA)
A report comparing a property to recently sold, similar homes to help determine market value.
D
Down Payment
The portion of the home price paid upfront in cash, reducing the amount financed by the loan.
DTI (Debt-to-Income Ratio)
Your total monthly debt payments divided by your gross monthly income — a key factor in loan approval.
E
Earnest Money
A deposit made by the buyer to demonstrate good faith when submitting an offer, credited toward closing costs.
Escrow
A neutral third party that holds funds and documents during a transaction until all conditions are met.
Equity
The difference between your home's market value and what you still owe on your mortgage.
F
Fixed-Rate Mortgage
A loan with an interest rate that stays the same for the entire loan term.
H
HOA (Homeowners Association)
An organization that manages shared amenities and rules in a community, funded by regular member dues.
L
Loan-to-Value (LTV)
The loan amount divided by the appraised value of the home, expressed as a percentage.
Lock-In Rate
An agreement that guarantees a specific interest rate for a set period while your loan is processed.
M
MLS (Multiple Listing Service)
A database used by real estate agents to share information about properties for sale.
Mortgage Insurance (PMI/MIP)
Insurance that protects the lender if a borrower defaults, typically required with a down payment under 20%.
P
Pre-Approval
A lender's conditional commitment to lend a specific amount, based on a review of your credit, income, and assets.
Principal
The original amount borrowed, not including interest.
T
Title Insurance
A policy that protects buyers and lenders against losses from defects in a property's title.
U
Underwriting
The lender's process of verifying your financial information and assessing risk before final loan approval.
FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

It depends on the loan program. Conventional loans can go as low as 3% down for qualified first-time buyers, FHA loans require 3.5%, and VA loans can offer 0% down for eligible veterans. We'll help you find the best fit for your situation.
Most purchase transactions close in 30-45 days from an accepted offer, though it can be faster with a strong pre-approval and responsive documentation. Cash purchases can close in as little as 7-14 days.
An initial pre-qualification can typically be done with a soft credit check that doesn't impact your score. A formal pre-approval involves a hard inquiry, which may cause a small, temporary dip.
Your agent represents you in finding a property and negotiating the purchase or sale. Your loan officer manages the financing side — from application through underwriting to funding. At Blue Venture, both work under one roof and coordinate directly.
While not always required by lenders, we strongly recommend a professional inspection for every purchase. It can uncover issues that affect your negotiating position or reveal costly repairs before you commit.
We prepare a comparative market analysis (CMA) using recently sold and currently listed comparable homes, adjusted for your property's condition, upgrades, and location, to recommend a data-backed list price.
