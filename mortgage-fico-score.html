Understanding Your FICO Score

Your FICO score is one of the biggest factors lenders use to determine your interest rate and loan eligibility. Here's how it works.

Score Ranges

What Your Score Means

Poor

300 – 579. May limit loan options or require a higher down payment.

Fair

580 – 669. Some loan programs, like FHA loans, may still be available.

Good

670 – 739. Generally qualifies for a solid range of conventional loan options.

Very Good

740 – 799. Often qualifies for more competitive interest rates.

Exceptional

800 – 850. Typically eligible for a lender's best available rates.

The Breakdown

What Affects Your Score

  1. Payment History (35%)

    Whether you've paid past credit accounts on time.

  2. Amounts Owed (30%)

    How much of your available credit you're currently using.

  3. Length of Credit History (15%)

    How long your credit accounts have been open.

  4. Credit Mix & New Credit (20%)

    The variety of credit types you have and how recently you've opened new accounts.

Tips

Ways to Strengthen Your Score

Pay on Time

Set up autopay or reminders so accounts are never accidentally missed.

Keep Balances Low

Try to use well under the available limit on credit cards.

Avoid New Accounts Before Applying

Opening new credit shortly before applying for a mortgage can temporarily lower your score.

Check Your Credit Report

Review your report for errors and dispute anything that looks incorrect.
